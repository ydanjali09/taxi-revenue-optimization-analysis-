**🚖 Taxi Revenue Optimization & Payment Behavior Analysis**

 **📌 Project Overview**

This project analyzes taxi trip data to identify revenue-driving factors, customer payment behavior, and operational trends. Using Python-based data analysis, data cleaning, visualization, and statistical hypothesis testing, the project provides actionable recommendations to improve revenue and fleet allocation decisions.

---

**🎯 Business Objective**

The primary goal of this project was to understand the factors influencing taxi revenue and determine whether customer payment methods impact fare and tipping behavior.

Key business questions addressed:

* Which payment method generates higher revenue?
* What are the peak demand hours for taxi services?
* Do airport trips generate higher fares than city trips?
* Which locations contribute the most revenue?
* How can fleet deployment be optimized?

 **📈 Statistical Analysis**
**Hypothesis Testing**

**Null Hypothesis (H₀):**
There is no significant difference in revenue generated by different payment methods.

**Alternative Hypothesis (H₁):**
There is a significant difference in revenue generated by different payment methods.

 **Method Used**

* Mann-Whitney U Test
* Significance Level: 0.05

**Result**

* p-value < 0.05
* Null hypothesis rejected
* Payment method significantly impacts revenue generation

---

🔍**Key Findings**

✅ Credit card customers generated higher average revenue.

✅ Airport trips showed higher average fares compared to standard city trips.

✅ Peak demand was concentrated during evening commuting hours.

✅ High-revenue pickup locations were identified for strategic fleet deployment.

✅ Payment behavior has a measurable impact on driver earnings.
